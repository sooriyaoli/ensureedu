4. Communications

The communications career field is about mastering the art of delivering a targeted message to diverse groups of people. Some positions in this field include:

  • Journalist
  • Copywriter
  • Communications manager
  • Public relations specialist
  • Meeting/event planner
  • Social media manager
  • Brand manager

Qualities needed

      Active listening

      Communication method

      Friendliness

      Confidence

      Sharing feedback

      Volume and clarity

      Empathy

      Respect

      Nonverbal cues

      Responsiveness

       Applied Communication: The general use of communication theories and practices to better understand and address real-world problems (Public Relations Specialists, Meeting/Event Planner, College Alumni & Development Officers, Media Planner, Social Media Manager)

       Communication Education: The application of communication theories in the classroom to create better modes of instruction. (Social and digital media, Public relations, Marketing and advertising, Human resources, Writing and publishing)

       Electronic & Digital Media: The different media platforms and technologies that are used in interpersonal and mass communications, how these technologies function, and their impact on information processes. (Digital media photographer, Video editor, Animator, Social media specialist, Graphic designer, Video game designer, Web analytics specialist, Web developer)

       Health Communication: Communication theories applied in healthcare settings. (Health Care Provider, Senior PR and Communication Manager, Data for health initiative)

       International & Intercultural Communication: Communicating across national and international borders and with people from different social, political, and cultural backgrounds. (International Education Coordinator, Translator., Intercultural Trainer, Refugee Counselor, Mediator)

       Interpersonal Communication: The complex processes of communication between individuals, including how body language and verbal cues are processed. (Public Relations, Advertising, Marketing, Corporate Public Affairs, Development, Media Analysis/Planning, Creative Directing, Writing/Editing)

 

       Legal Communication: Communication theory applied in the context of the legal system, courtrooms, and other areas of the law and criminal justice. (Company lawyer, Legal Assistant, Senior Legal Counsel)

 

       Mass Communication & Media Literacy: The uses of print, radio, television, and online technologies to reach large audiences, and how mass audiences process and understand the messages they receive. (Content Writing and Generation, Public Relations, Journalism, TV Correspondence, Fashion Photography, Advertising, Film Industry, Event Management, Legal Affairs, Online Media, Digital Marketing, etc)

 

 

       Mediation and Dispute Resolution: Theories of conflict and argumentation and how they can be applied to resolving disputes and forging mutual understanding. (Arbitrator, Mediator, Conciliator, Contract Negotiation Manager, Ombudsmen, Appraisers, Examiners, Claims Adjusters, Investigators, Policy Analyst, Fact-finders)

 

       Organizational Communication: Theories of how people communicate within an organization and the application of these theories to improving the function of businesses and other types of organizations. (Account manager, Activities director, Administrative aide, Admissions counselor, Alumni coordinator, Arts administrator, Assistant to the college president)

 

   Performance Studies: The study of performers, audiences, and texts in the context of popular entertainment and other forms of public performance. (Broadway/Off-Broadway Actor, Production Manager/Producer, Stage Management, Casting Director)

 

       Political Communication: The use of communication tools among citizen groups, politicians, the news media, and other actors in the political ecosphere. (social media managers, creative specialists, account managers, copywriters, online content creators, television reporters or producers, investigative or political journalists, foreign correspondents)

   Public Address: The use of rhetorical tools to convince, persuade, and influence people through public presentations and oratory. (Tour guide, Teacher, Actor, Health educator, Broadcaster, Mediator, Training specialist, Purchasing manager)

 

       Public Relations: The management and mediation of relationships between organizations and various sectors of the public using the tools of communication. (Advertising account executive, Advertising copywriter, Event manager, Marketing executive, Media researcher)

 

       Rhetorical Criticism: The analysis and critique of rhetoric and its use to convey messages in the public and private spheres. (web authoring, copywriting, speech writing, technical writing, grant writing, science writing, journalism, teaching, law, government and business)

       Small Group Communication: The use of communication theory to better understand the interactions of small groups and improve communication in these groups.

       Visual Communication: Visual data and its uses in communicating messages and ideas.(Art director, Commercial Artist, Game designer/Developer etc.)
